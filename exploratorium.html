Frequently Asked Questions

How do you know that nobody has seen this star before?

In consultation with academic research astronomers across the globe, we’ve developed a very thorough process for identifying which stars fit the bill. By combining information from an assortment of databases on every star, we ensure that each one we print out has never been noted as scientifically interesting, never been mentioned in any published research, and is faint enough that earlier surveys couldn't capture it clearly. In short: these stars are as unimportant and actively ignored as possible.

Will I own this star? Can I name it?

No, and companies or sites that claim otherwise are scamming you. What's important about your relationship to this star is not ownership, but the unique experience of being the first human to ever see it. These stars remain part of our shared universal heritage, but your connection to it is personal and profound — a moment in cosmic history that belongs only to you.

Can I see this star myself? How can I learn more about it?

The star is too far out in space to see with the naked eye or even most amateur telescopes, but each First Light info sheet comes with a map of where it is in the sky among visible constellations, an interactive website to check out its neighbors, and more. You'll also receive detailed information about your star's position, magnitude, and estimated distance from Earth, allowing you to understand its place in our galaxy.
